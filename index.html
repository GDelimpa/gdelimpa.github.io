Research Profile

I am a Postdoctoral researcher at TU Eindhoven (Control Systems Technology group, Mechanical Engineering). I am active in the field of control theory and its intersections with optimization and formal methods. My specific research interests span the following:

  • The interface of formal methods and dynamical systems, including hybrid systems (systems with both continuous and discrete dynamics), finite-state models of dynamical systems (formal abstractions), verification and control of dynamical systems through finite abstractions, formal languages and automata.
  • Stochastic dynamics and control, including Markov decision processes, reinforcement learning, and information theory in control.
  • The interface of optimization and control, including nonsmooth dynamics, optimization-based control for safety-critical tasks (control-barrier functions, projection-based control), and feedback optimization (optimization algorithms interconnected with dynamical systems, online optimization of dynamical systems).

Academic Background

I obtained the Diploma (joint BSc. and MSc. degree) in Electrical and Computer Engineering, from the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA) in 2017. I obtained my PhD, Cum Laude (top 5% in the Netherlands), from Delft University of Technology, in 2022.
